"Computer science is no more about computers than astronomy is about telescopes." - Edsger Dijkstra

I'm a junior at Tufts University studying Computer Science and Cognitive & Brain Sciences. My academic focus is at the intersection of algorithms and cognitive science, especially machine learning and artificial intelligence.

My current interest is in the constantly changing definition we assign to AI as computers challenge our assumptions and intuitions about which capacities are uniquely human. This interest has led me to explore the fields of philosophy, psychology, cognitive neuroscience, and of course, computer science.

After college I hope to get a job as a full-time software engineer, making use of machine intelligence principles and an understanding of human behavior and cognition.
